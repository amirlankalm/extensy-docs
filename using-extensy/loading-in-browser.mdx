# Seeing Your Work in Action

Before you publish to the world, you'll want to test your extension locally. This guide will show you how to load your generated ZIP into Google Chrome using **Developer Mode**.

## Loading Your Extension

Follow these steps to get your first extension up and running.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download and Unzip Your Extension">
    Click the **Download ZIP** from the Extensy dashboard and save the folder to your computer. Extract the ZIP file into a regular folder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Chrome Extensions">
    Open Chrome and navigate to `chrome://extensions` or click the puzzle icon in your toolbar and select **Manage extensions**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle Developer Mode">
    In the top-right corner, find the **Developer mode** toggle and turn it on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Load Unpacked">
    Click the **Load unpacked** button in the top-left menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Your Extension Folder">
    A file browser will appear. Select the unzipped folder containing your extension files.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Testing Your Extension

1. **Check the Toolbar**: If your extension has a popup, find the icon in the toolbar. You may need to click the puzzle icon and pin your extension for easier access.
2. **Open a Webpage**: If your extension interacts with content, visit a page that matches your prompt's target (e.g., [wikipedia.org](https://wikipedia.org)).
3. **Trigger Your Action**: Click the toolbar icon or look for the injection on the page.

## Reloading After Changes

If you manually edit a file (`popup.js`, `manifest.json`, etc.), you don't need to re-upload. 

1. Save your file changes in your code editor.
2. Go back to `chrome://extensions`.
3. Click the **Reload icon (↺)** on your extension's card.
4. Refresh your current tab and test again!

<Note>
  **Important Note**: Chrome will not automatically "hot-reload" your extension. You must manually click the reload button on the extensions management page.
</Note>

## Other Chromium Browsers

While these steps are centered on Chrome, you can also load your Extensy-generated extensions into other Chromium-based browsers like **Brave**, **Edge**, **Opera**, or **Vivaldi**. 

Just look for the equivalent `extensions` management page and follow the same "Developer mode" and "Load unpacked" process.

<Tip>
  If your extension fails to load, check the **Errors** button on your extension's card in `chrome://extensions`. It will give you a detailed breakdown of which line or file caused the issue!
</Tip>
