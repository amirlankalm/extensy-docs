# Powering Your Generations

Extensy uses a simple, transparent credit-based system to manage the AI powerful enough to build entire browser extensions in seconds. This guide explains how to get, use, and manage your credits.

## What is a Credit?

A credit represents one complete AI-powered generation. When you click **Generate**, our pipeline analyzes your prompt, architects the code, and packages a functional ZIP.

- **1 Complete Generation = 1 Credit**
- **Successful Output Only**: Credits are only deducted if our system produces a valid, downloadable ZIP file.

## Check Your Balance

You can see your remaining credits at any time in the top-right corner of the Extensy dashboard. 

<Info>
  **Pro-tip**: Hover over your credit count to see when your next monthly grant will be awarded.
</Info>

## Consumption Rules

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does every prompt cost a credit?">
    Yes. Every time you click "Generate" to build a new extension or iterate on an existing one, one credit is consumed. 
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if the generation fails?">
    If our AI service runs into an error or fails to produce a ZIP, your credit is immediately and automatically returned to your balance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do credits roll over?">
    No. Credits granted through our monthly plans do not roll over to the next month. Make sure to use your monthly quota!
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I get a refund for a bad output?">
    Because AI generation is computationally expensive, we cannot offer refunds for credits spent on prompts that didn't produce exactly what you expected. We recommend using our **Prompting Guide** to get it right the first time.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Wasting Credits: How to Avoid It

Before you click "Generate," ask yourself these questions:

1. **Is my prompt specific?** (Does it mention which site, what color, and what action?)
2. **Have I checked the Prompting Guide?** (Are there better templates I could use?)
3. **Is this possible for an extension?** (Am I asking for something a browser sandbox won't allow?)

<Tip>
  If you're unsure about a complex prompt, start with a simpler version and iterate. It's often cheaper to refine than to start with a massive, error-prone request.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  **Important Note**: Once a generation is complete and the ZIP is generated, the credit is considered spent. We cannot undo or "un-generate" an output.
</Warning>
