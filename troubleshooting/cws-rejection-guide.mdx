# Overcoming Store Rejections

A rejection from the Chrome Web Store (CWS) is not the end. It's simply a sign that your extension needs a few adjustments to follow Google's current policies. If you receive a rejection email, this guide will help you fix the most common issues.

## Understanding the Rejection Email

Google will always cite a specific **Policy** (e.g., "Permissions" or "Misleading Metadata") and provide a link to the relevant rule. Read the email carefully, as it often includes a helpful hint about where your extension failed.

## Common Rejection Reasons

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="01. Misleading Metadata">
    **Cause**: Your extension's name or description doesn't clearly state what it does, or it uses trademarked names in a confusing way.
    **Fix**: Use a descriptive name like "Enhancer for Instagram" instead of just "Instagram". Rewrite your store description to lead with the primary feature.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="02. Excessive Permissions">
    **Cause**: Your extension's `manifest.json` requests permissions (like `history` or `<all_urls>`) that its code never actually uses.
    **Fix**: This is the most common reason for rejection. Remove every permission that isn't essential. Use `activeTab` instead of `tabs` if possible.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="03. Missing Privacy Policy">
    **Cause**: You requested a sensitive permission (like `cookies` or `storage`) but didn't provide a link to a privacy policy in the console.
    **Fix**: Create a simple privacy policy on your website or use a generator. Link to it in the **Privacy** tab of the Developer Console.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="04. Deceptive Installation">
    **Cause**: The extension's behavior is unexpected or was not described in the store listing.
    **Fix**: Be transparent. If your extension changes the user's search engine or homepage, it **must** be explicitly stated in the first sentence of your description.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="05. Repetitive Content">
    **Cause**: Your extension is too similar to one already in the store, or it's a direct copy of a popular tool.
    **Fix**: Add a unique feature or rebranding that sets your tool apart. Avoid "Ad Blockers" that don't bring anything new to the ecosystem.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="06. Prohibited Functionality">
    **Cause**: Includes ad injection, malware, or search hijacking.
    **Fix**: Ensure your code only performs the actions described. Extensy never generates malicious code, so this usually happens if you've added poor manual refinements.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="07. Insufficient Permission Justification">
    **Cause**: In the Developer Console, you provided a vague reason (e.g., "Needed for functionality") for a major permission.
    **Fix**: Be ultra-specific: "The storage permission is used to save the user's localized price-highlighting color across sessions."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="08. Remote Code Execution">
    **Cause**: Your extension uses `eval()` or attempts to fetch a script from a remote server.
    **Fix**: Manifest V3 prohibits all remote code. All logic must be contained in the ZIP file you upload.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="09. Inactive Extension">
    **Cause**: You submitted a "placeholder" with no real features.
    **Fix**: Finish building your extension. Google will not approve a "Coming Soon" or non-functional tool.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  **Pro-tip**: After any rejection, **increment your version number** in `manifest.json` (e.g., from `1.0.0` to `1.0.1`) before re-uploading the ZIP. Chrome will not accept an update with the same version number as your previous submission.
</Tip>

## How to Appeal a Rejection

If you believe your extension was rejected in error, you can reply directly to the rejection email. Be polite, cite the policy, and explain how your extension follows the rules.

<Warning>
  **Important Note**: Once a rejection is received, do not simply resubmit the same ZIP. Google's review queue is automated—resubmitting without changes will lead to an immediate second rejection or, in some cases, a developer account suspension!
</Warning>

</Note>
