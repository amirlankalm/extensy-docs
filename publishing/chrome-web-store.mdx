# Launching to the World

Your assets are ready, your account is verified, and your extension is tested. It's time to submit. This guide will walk you through the final steps in the **Chrome Web Store Developer Console**.

## Submission Walkthrough

Follow these steps carefully to ensure a smooth review process.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log In to Console">
    Navigate to the [Chrome Web Store Developer Console](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/devconsole) and log in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload Your ZIP">
    Click the **+ New Item** button and upload the functional ZIP file generated by Extensy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in Store Listing">
    Provide your extension's name, short description, and long description. Ensure these match your prepared assets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload Visual Assets">
    Upload your 128x128px icon and at least one screenshot (1280x800px).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Category and Language">
    Choose the most relevant category (e.g., Productivity) and the primary language of your extension.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Declare Privacy and Permissions">
    Go to the **Privacy** tab. You must declare every permission your extension uses. You must also include a link to your privacy policy (you can use the bespoke, hosted privacy policy URL generated by Extensy's AI!).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Distribution Settings">
    In the **Distribution** tab, choose if your extension is **Public** (visible to all), **Unlisted** (only via link), or **Private** (test group only).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for Review">
    Review everything in the preview window one last time and click **Submit for Review**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Permission Justifications

This is where most first-time publishers get stuck. Google wants to know **why** your extension needs specific permissions.

| Permission | Example Justification |
| :--- | :--- |
| **storage** | "To save user preferences for theme and localized settings." |
| **tabs** | "To identify the active tab for price highlighting functionality." |
| **activeTab** | "To execute scripts on the current page when the user clicks the extension." |

<Tip>
  **Pro-tip**: Be honest and concise. If your justification is vague (e.g., "needed for functionality"), you are much more likely to be rejected.
</Tip>

## What Happens Next?

Once submitted, your extension status will change to **"Pending Review"**.

- **Automation Phase**: Google's systems scan your code for malware and policy violations.
- **Human Phase**: A reviewer may manually check your extension's UI and its permission justifications.
- **Completion**: Once approved, your status will change to **"Published"**, and you'll receive an email notification.

<Info>
  **Typical Timeline**: Expect a **1 to 3 business day** wait. First-time submissions or extensions with broad permissions (like `<all_urls>`) may take up to a week.
</Info>

## Handling Rejections

Don't panic. If your extension is rejected, Google will send you an email with the **exact policy** violated.

1. Read the policy carefully (Google usually provides a link).
2. Fix the specific issue cited.
3. Increment your `version` in `manifest.json`.
4. Re-upload the ZIP and resubmit.

<Warning>
  **Prohibited Actions**: Do not use `eval()` or load external scripts. Manifest V3 strictly prohibits remote code execution to prevent security vulnerabilities. Every line of code must be in your ZIP.
</Warning>
