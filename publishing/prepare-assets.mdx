# Shine in the Store

A functional extension is not enough. To attract users and build trust, your store listing needs high-quality visual and written assets. This checklist covers everything you'll need before you click **Submit**.

## Checklist: Core Assets

- [ ] **Extension ZIP**: Your Extensy-generated, tested, and unedited or refined ZIP package.
- [ ] **Store Icon**: 128x128px PNG. This is the main icon users see while browsing the store.
- [ ] **Screenshots**: At least 1 (up to 5). Extensy AI will generate promotional slides for you, or you can supply your own (1280x800px).
- [ ] **Short Description**: Max 132 characters. This is the summary shown in search results.
- [ ] **Detailed Description**: Explain what your extension does, who it's for, and how to use it.
- [ ] **Category**: Choose the most relevant category (e.g., Productivity, Shopping, or Developer Tools).
- [ ] **Privacy Policy & TOS**: Required if your extension collects any user data. Extensy AI generates these automatically as native documentation URLs for your project!

## Designing Your Icons

Your icons represent your brand. While Extensy provides basic ones, we recommend creating a custom 128x128px icon for your store listing. 

<Tip>
  **Pro-tip**: Use your extension's icon from the `icons` folder as your store icon for consistency. If you're a developer, you can use tools like Figma or Canva to refine it.
</Tip>

## AI-Generated Promo Slides & Screenshots

Users want to see the extension in action. Extensy now auto-generates beautiful, CWS-ready promotional slides in our interactive modal viewer, which you can publish directly. If you want to take your own screenshots, capture:

1. **Popup View**: Show the extension's popup UI clearly.
2. **On-Page Injection**: If your extension modifies a webpage, show the "before" and "after".
3. **Action Shots**: Show a user interacting with a button or a badge.

## Writing Your Store Listing

Your description should be clear, professional, and highlight the value.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Short Description Sample">
    "Track prices effortlessly. This extension highlights local currency symbols and calculates the total sum on any e-commerce page automatically."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Detailed Description Template">
    **What it does**: A clear 1-2 sentence overview.  
    **Key features**: Bullet point list of your main functionality.  
    **How to use**: Step-by-step instructions (e.g., "1. Go to any site. 2. Click the icon.").  
    **Privacy**: State what data you access (if any) and why.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Privacy Policy Template (Minimal)">
    "This extension does not collect or store any personal data. All processing is done locally on your machine. We do not use remote servers or trackers."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Info>
  **Note on Privacy Policies**: If your extension collects any user data, you **must** provide a link to a privacy policy website. Extensy automatically writes bespoke Privacy and Terms of Service Markdown documents tailored to your extension's codebase and hosts them dynamically on your project's unique URL (e.g., `/projects/[id]/privacy`), saving you the hassle of hosting legal docs yourself!
</Info>

## Justifying Permissions

For every permission requested in your `manifest.json` (like `storage` or `tabs`), you should be ready to write a one-sentence justification in the developer dashboard. 

> **Example**: "Storage permission is used to save your preferred theme color across browser sessions."

<Warning>
  **Prohibited Content**: Avoid using trademarked brand names in your extension's name unless you are the owner. Instead of "Facebook Enhancer", use "Enhancer for Facebook".
</Warning>
