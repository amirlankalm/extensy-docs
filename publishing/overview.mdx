# From Local to Global

You've built and tested your extension locally, and it's perfect. Now it's time to share it with the world. This guide will walk you through the entire journey of publishing to the **Chrome Web Store (CWS)**.

## The Publishing Journey

Publishing to the official store involves four key stages.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare Assets">
    Generate your icons, take screenshots, and write a compelling description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create Developer Account">
    Register with Google to become a verified extension developer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for Review">
    Upload your ZIP and assets to the Chrome Web Store Developer Console for approval.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post-Publish Management">
    Monitor your installs, respond to reviews, and push updates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Stage 01. Prepare Assets

Before you open the developer console, make sure you have your metadata ready. This includes your functional extension ZIP, a high-quality 128x128px icon, and at least one screenshot of your extension in action.

## Stage 02. Create Developer Account

To publish to the Chrome Web Store, you need a Google Developer Account. This requires a one-time registration fee of **$5 USD**. Once paid, you can publish up to 20 extensions initially.

## Stage 03. Submit for Review

Once your assets are ready and your account is active, you'll upload your ZIP and fill out the store listing. A Chrome Web Store reviewer (and automated systems) will then check your extension for policy compliance and security.

<Info>
  **Typical Timeline**: Most reviews take between **1 and 3 business days**, though some may take longer if your extension requests broad permissions.
</Info>
