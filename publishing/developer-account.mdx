# Your Entry into the Store

To publish to the world via the Chrome Web Store, you need a **Google Developer Account**. This guide will show you how to register and verify your identity as an extension developer.

## Registering to Publish

Follow these steps to set up your account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Developer Console">
    Open the [Chrome Web Store Developer Console](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/devconsole).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign In with Google">
    Sign in with a Google account you plan to use for your long-term developer identity. We recommend a dedicated Google account for your projects.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accept the Agreement">
    Review and accept the Google Chrome Web Store Developer Agreement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pay the Registration Fee">
    There is a one-time registration fee of **$5 USD** (Google Pay or credit card). This is not a recurring subscription; it's a one-time cost to verify your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Account Active">
    Once the payment is processed, your developer account is active. You can now publish up to 20 extensions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Important Recommendations

- **Use a Dedicated Account**: Avoid using your personal primary Gmail. Use an account that can be shared with team members if your project grows.
- **Security First**: Enable **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on your developer account. If your account is compromised, your extensions and their users are at risk.
- **Company Identity**: If you're building as a company, select "Organization" during signup to ensure the developer name matches your brand.

## The Developer Dashboard

Once your account is set up, you'll have access to the dashboard. Here, you can:

- Upload new extensions.
- Monitor your installs and active user counts.
- View and respond to user reviews.
- See crash reports and performance data.
- Manage your privacy settings.

<Warning>
  **Losing Access**: If you lose access to the Google account tied to your developer console, you may lose the ability to update your published extensions. Store your login credentials securely!
</Warning>

## Account Limits

For new accounts, Google typically has a **20-extension limit**. If you need to publish more, you can request an increase from the support team within the dashboard.
